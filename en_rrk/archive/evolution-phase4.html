Phase 4 — Convergence of Stable Structures

Phase 4 describes the convergent stabilization of previously formed complex emergent patterns. Through the application of the convergence operator, unstable patterns are transformed into consistent, durable meta‑structures.

Core Characteristics of Phase 4

  • Stabilization of emergent patterns
  • Formation of consistent meta‑structures
  • Reduction of structural instability
  • Beginning of architectural coherence
  • Foundation for transformation and architecture (Phase 5)

Structural Role

The convergence phase forms the first level in which complex patterns become permanently stable. It creates the structural coherence required to enable full architectural transformations in Phase 5.
