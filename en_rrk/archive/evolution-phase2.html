Phase 2 — Meta‑Structural Emergence

Phase 2 describes the first stable emergence above the recursive base level. Through repeated recursion, meta‑structures arise that possess new functional properties and enable transitions between recursive units.

Core Characteristics of Phase 2

  • First stable meta‑structures
  • Emergence through repeated recursion
  • Transitions between recursive units
  • Beginning of functional pattern formation
  • Foundation for more complex emergence processes

Structural Role

The meta‑structural level forms the first layer above the base level that is no longer purely recursive. It enables the formation of stable patterns that later lead to complex emergent structures in Phase 3.
