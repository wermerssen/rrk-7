Structural Units

The structural units of the RRK framework define the fundamental building blocks from which recursive, emergent, and convergent processes are composed. They represent the elementary objects of the ontological architecture.

Unit 1 — Recursive Unit

The recursive unit is the elementary base component of the RRK framework. It serves as the starting point for iterative transformations and forms the foundation of all higher structures.

Unit 2 — Meta‑Structural Unit

Meta‑structural units arise through repeated recursion and form the first emergent layer above the base level.

Unit 3 — Emergence Structure

Emergence structures include patterns and configurations generated by the emergence operator. These structures cannot be directly derived from the base level.

Unit 4 — Convergence Structure

Convergence structures stabilize emergent patterns and transform them into consistent meta‑structures. They form the basis for higher architectural layers.

Unit 5 — Transformation Structure

Transformation structures describe the evolution of stable meta‑structures and enable the formation of complex architectural patterns within the RRK framework.
