Ontological Dynamics

The ontological dynamics of the RRK framework describe the fundamental movement, transition, and transformation processes within the recursive, emergent, and convergent architecture. They define how structures evolve across levels.

Dynamics 1 — Recursive Dynamics

Recursive dynamics describe the iterative application of a structure to itself. They form the foundation of all recursive processes and transformations.

Dynamics 2 — Meta‑Dynamics

Meta‑dynamics describe the transitions between recursive units and meta‑structural levels. They enable the formation of higher functional patterns.

Dynamics 3 — Emergence Dynamics

Emergence dynamics encompass the processes through which new patterns arise that cannot be directly derived from the base level. They form the foundation for complex emergent structures.

Dynamics 4 — Convergence Dynamics

Convergence dynamics stabilize emergent patterns and transform them into consistent meta‑structures. They form the basis for higher architectural layers.

Dynamics 5 — Transformation Dynamics

Transformation dynamics describe the structural evolution of stable meta‑structures. They enable the formation of complex architectural patterns and form the transition to the complete RRK‑7 architecture.
