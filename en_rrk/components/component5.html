Component 5 — Structural Transformation

Structural transformation is the fifth core component of the RRK framework. It describes the mechanism through which convergent meta‑structures (Component 4) are transformed into new functional configurations.

Definition

A structural transformation is a process that converts stable meta‑structures into new configurations while preserving the underlying recursive logic. It forms the basis for the development of complex architectural patterns within the RRK framework.

Role in the RRK Framework

  • the evolution of stable meta‑structures
  • the formation of complex architectural patterns

It represents the transition from stable meta‑structures to dynamic, functionally extended systems.
